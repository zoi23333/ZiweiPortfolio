Introduction

The project is an advanced system integrating a VR platform and web-based dashboards, aimed at enhancing fitness and rehabilitation for individuals with upper-limb impairments. By collaborating with stakeholders, the team creates an immersive and engaging environment, making the daily exercise for main users more efficient and motivating.

Outcomes

+ Developed an integrated VR system tailored for people with upper-limb impairments.
+ Validated design through user testing, leading to enhanced usability.
+ Established a foundation for future iterations and scalability of the project.

Previews Systems
The previous systems, designed and developed by our research group, form the foundation upon which our current project is built. However, these systems have limitations that make them unsuitable for our project:
  • Narrow User Focus: Current systems primarily target individuals with rehabilitation needs, such as stroke patients, but there is a growing demand from people with permanent upper-limb impairments who seek fitness exercises to improve their strength and motivation.
  • Fragmented Systems: Previous research efforts resulted in three separate systems designed for different stakeholders, but these systems have not been technically integrated.
  • Inconsistent Design: The visual design and interactive design across these systems are inconsistent, leading to a fragmented experience.

How might we design a product system that integrates human digital twin and VR to effectively engage all user groups within a limited timeframe?
User Requirements
The personas for the three types of users‚Äîprimary user (people with upper-limb impairments and need to exercise upper-limbs), physical therapist, and third-party caregivers‚Äîare based on thorough research and represent each group's challenges, and aspirations, highlighting the key wishes that the design must address.

Impairments Scope
By consulting with physical therapists and experts in the related field, we identified the scope of impairments for the primary users by Ability Prompt Card.
Two important facts about upper limb impairments:
  • Lower Limb impairments: Upper limb impairments are often accompanied by lower limb impairments, and individuals who use wheelchairs daily require strengthening their arms.
  • Cognitive Issues: Upper limb impairments can also be associated with cognitive issues, whether congenital or caused by disease or injury.
Design Quality
The attributes of this project require us to adopt the following approach:
User-Centric Design
The design should place users at the core, ensuring the system is intuitive and engaging, meeting their needs through constant feedback and iteration.
Accessible Design
Applying WCAG 2.2 and WebAIM guidelines to ensure the system is accessible to all, addressing various user impairments effectively.
Consistent Design
Unifying visual style, interaction patterns, and layout, to create a cohesive experience in the three platforms of the whole systems.

06 / Future Directions & Reflections

Potential Future Improvement
  • Customization vs. Broader Testing: To better meet user needs, two approaches can be considered.
    (a) Customizing the system for specific groups like students in specialized institutions or stroke patients.
    (b) Conducting broader user testing with a more diverse group of users, ensuring the system is effective for a wider audience.
  • Hardware Accessibility: Improve the design of VR hardware (headsets and controllers) to make it more user-friendly for individuals with physical impairments.
  • Feedback from 3rd-Party Caregivers: Incorporate feedback from 3rd-party caregivers to better understand their needs and improve the system's overall support for primary users.
  • Comprehensive Range of Motion: The range of motion tracking should be more comprehensive to provide accurate feedback on user progress. Enhancing the sensors that collect motion data would improve the user experience.
Reflections
  • Communication & Collaboration: Working on this complex project with developers and stakeholders showed the importance of clear communication and simplifying technical concepts to keep everyone aligned.
  • Iterative Workflow: Design and development aren‚Äôt linear processes. Continuous iteration, feedback, and problem-solving are essential to improving the system.
