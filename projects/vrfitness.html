Introduction

The project is an advanced system integrating a VR platform and web-based dashboards, aimed at enhancing fitness and rehabilitation for individuals with upper-limb impairments. By collaborating with stakeholders, the team creates an immersive and engaging environment, making the daily exercise for main users more efficient and motivating.

Outcomes

+ Developed an integrated VR system tailored for people with upper-limb impairments.
+ Validated design through user testing, leading to enhanced usability.

01 / Background

This project is built upon the following key foundations:
Upper Limb Impairments
Overview:
Addresses movement limitations in arms, crucial for fitness design.
Reason for Focus:
Essential for improving mobility and quality of life for individuals with impairments.
Human Digital Twin
Overview:
‍Digital representation of real-time human data.
Reason for Use:
‍Provides detailed feedback, enhancing personalized therapy for people with movement limitations.
VR Technology
Overview:
Immersive virtual environments for enhanced engagement in fitness.
Reason for Focus:
Ensures inclusivity, allowing people with impairments to benefit from new technologies.
As for physical devices, The project relies on:
  • Motion Sensors: Utilized for tracking user movements.
  • VR Headset & Controllers: Allow users to interact with the virtual environment.
  • Exercise Equipment: Includes dumbbells, hand cyclers, and wheelchairs, each offering different levels of physical engagement and tracking complexity within the VR system.
The limitation in motion sensors presents a challenge:
Challenge
The motion sensors can only detect basic movements, without capturing 3D depth.
Current Need
Creating a human digital twin requires precise user data and activity tracking.
Who Are We Designing For
  • Primary User: People with upper-limb impairments and need to exercise upper-limbs.
  • Physical therapists: Assist primary users with their daily exercise routines.
  • 3rd-party caregivers: Typically family members or guardians of the primary users.
Issues of Previews Systems
The previous systems, designed and developed by our research group, form the foundation upon which our current project is built. However, these systems have limitations that make them unsuitable for our current needs.
  • Narrow User Focus: Current systems primarily target individuals with rehabilitation needs, such as stroke patients, but there is a growing demand from people with permanent upper-limb impairments who seek fitness exercises to improve their strength and motivation.
  • Fragmented Systems: Previous research efforts resulted in three separate systems designed for different stakeholders, but these systems have not been technically integrated.
  • Inconsistent Design: The visual design and interactive design across these systems are inconsistent, leading to a fragmented experience.
Time Constraint
The time constraint also brings a challenge to the project:
Challenge
We need to deliver a fully functional digital system that meets the needs.
Time Constraint
We have just four months to scope, design, develop, and validate the system.
Who Are We Designing with
This project is a joint master's thesis project conducted in DTU Management. It builds upon previous research and design efforts by the research group. The design and user testing were further supported by the physical therapists and students of the special school Maglemosen.

02 / Problem Definition

How might we design a product system that integrates human digital twin and VR to effectively engage all user groups within a limited timeframe?
User Requirements
The personas for the three types of users—primary user (people with upper-limb impairments and need to exercise upper-limbs), physical therapist, and third-party caregivers—are based on thorough research and represent each group's challenges, and aspirations, highlighting the key wishes that the design must address.

Impairments Scope
By consulting with physical therapists and experts in the related field, we identified the scope of impairments for the primary users by Ability Prompt Card.
Two important facts about upper limb impairments:
  • Lower Limb impairments: Upper limb impairments are often accompanied by lower limb impairments, and individuals who use wheelchairs daily require strengthening their arms.
  • Cognitive Issues: Upper limb impairments can also be associated with cognitive issues, whether congenital or caused by disease or injury.
Design Quality
The attributes of this project require us to adopt the following approach:
User-Centric Design
The design should place users at the core, ensuring the system is intuitive and engaging, meeting their needs through constant feedback and iteration.
Accessible Design
Applying WCAG 2.2 and WebAIM guidelines to ensure the system is accessible to all, addressing various user impairments effectively.
Consistent Design
Unifying visual style, interaction patterns, and layout, to create a cohesive experience in the three platforms of the whole systems.

6 User Testing

We conducted user testing at a specialized education and activity center for individuals with various disabilities and special needs, including autism and ADHD. The center's administrators and therapists helped us select nine participants.
Participant Overview
Of the participants, six used wheelchairs daily, three had reduced hand functionality, and one had tremors that affected their motor control. These participants engaged in testing using four VR headsets in a fitness rehabilitation room, where we explored the effectiveness of our solution.
Procudure
  • ⏱️ Duration: 5 days over 3 weeks, with each student attending up to 5 sessions, each lasting around 30 minutes.
  • 👥 Participants: 9 students, 6 therapists. Of the students, six used wheelchairs daily, three had reduced hand functionality, and one had tremors that affected their motor control.  
  • 🎮 Activities: Students alternated between playing VR cycling games (Hand cycle) and dumbbell exercises (Jump Jump). The Wheelchair Race game was removed from daily activities due to discomfort and cybersickness.
  • 😃 Methods: Students rated their experience using an emoji scale and participated in final interviews. Therapists provided feedback through interviews and surveys. Daily observations and valuable conversations were also recorded.
