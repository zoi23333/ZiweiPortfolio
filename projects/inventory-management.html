01 / The Problem

How might we optimize inventory management in retail to enhance efficiency and customer satisfaction?
Retail shops frequently encounter significant challenges in inventory management, especially for fast-selling items. Traditionally, by the time it's recognized that stock levels are critically low, it's usually too late to replenish in time due to logistical and processing timeframes. This results in lost sales opportunities and customer dissatisfaction.
Our team aims to meet the specific needs of a renowned Chinese retail company while developing a product with universality and standardization. The goal is to evolve this into a scalable SaaS product that enhances inventory management across the retail industry, optimizing efficiency and customer satisfaction to support future business growth.

02 / Identify the Problem

Understanding User Pain Points and Needs
Through meetings with our customers and stakeholders, and by reviewing the materials they provided, I have identified the following pain points. These were further refined based on suggestions from the product manager.
Stock Monitoring
Customers rely on data tables to manually assess and manage stock levels, which is time-consuming. They need an automated system that provides an instant overview of stock status, enabling quick insights without the need for manual analysis.
Ordering Process
Customers assess inventory by tallying stock in spreadsheets to identify replenishment needs. Orders are then manually placed and emailed to warehouses or suppliers, necessitating a streamlined, automated ordering system to enhance efficiency and accuracy.
Supply Chain Delays
Replenishment delays often result from extended supplier preparation and transit times, preventing timely restocking. A predictive system is needed to anticipate these delays and facilitate earlier ordering, ensuring consistent stock availability.
Mind Map
I collaborated with the product manager using Xmind to develop a mind map that organizes the product's essential functions. Through subsequent meetings with customers and developers, we refined this mind map, confirmed the project’s direction, and laid the groundwork for upcoming wireframes.

04 / Design Handover

Facilitating Seamless UI Implementation
In this phase, I provided developers with an Interaction Design Specification that included a Figma-drafted prototype and detailed interaction guidelines, such as pagination and list scrolling behaviors. Alongside the product manager, I led prototype presentation meetings to ensure developers thoroughly understood the interface and interaction designs. This allowed for seamless development using Figma’s developer mode, ensuring a clear understanding of the UI’s functionality and facilitating a smooth design-to-development transition.
